Institutional Adoption Guide — Structural Onboarding Specification  
Scope: Staged institutional onboarding and coordination sequencing procedures for the Governance Coordination Potent.  
Version: 1.1 (Revised)

This file defines staged onboarding mechanics only.  
It does not establish governance reform, institutional restructuring, or policy transformation.

---

## 1️⃣ Staged Institutional Onboarding Model

### 1.1 Stage 0 — Internal Review

**Operational Scope**

- Review of governance charter structural core
- Documentation alignment verification
- Identification of applicable tier scope

**Parameter Scope**

- No active parameter modification
- Parameter registry draft configuration

**Role Scope**

- Preliminary role designation
- Role separation mapping

**Documentation Requirements**

- Internal review log
- Structural readiness checklist
- Role registry draft

No external coordination required at this stage.

---

### 1.2 Stage 1 — Limited Pilot Implementation

**Operational Scope**

- Activation of governance structure within a single unit
- Controlled parameter registry implementation

**Parameter Scope**

- Parameter definitions within declared caps
- Version registry initiation

**Role Scope**

- Active assignment of Coordinator, Auditor, Maintainer, Registrar roles
- Role separation enforcement

**Documentation Requirements**

- Decision log activation
- Parameter version log
- Audit schedule declaration

Scope remains limited to single operational unit.

---

### 1.3 Stage 2 — Cross-Unit Coordination

**Operational Scope**

- Coordination across multiple units within defined scope
- Structured alignment of parameter registries

**Parameter Scope**

- Alignment within declared cross-unit caps
- Version synchronization across participating units

**Role Scope**

- Cross-unit role registry coordination
- Structured review routing active

**Documentation Requirements**

- Cross-unit reconciliation logs
- Version alignment records
- Structured dispute routing registry

Participation remains voluntary and deployment-defined.

---

### 1.4 Stage 3 — Tier-Level Coordination

**Operational Scope**

- Coordination across defined tiers
- Parameter cap layering active

**Parameter Scope**

- Tier-based cap definitions applied prospectively
- Cross-tier version registry alignment

**Role Scope**

- Tier-level role coordination registry
- Structured decision routing across tiers

**Documentation Requirements**

- Tier coordination log
- Parameter layering documentation
- Audit coordination registry

No mandatory expansion beyond declared scope.

---

## 2️⃣ Onboarding Preconditions

Prior to Stage 1 activation, the following MUST be confirmed:

- Governance Charter Structural Core documented
- Parameter registry framework established
- Role assignment registry documented
- Audit readiness checklist completed
- Log infrastructure configured and verified

All preconditions MUST be recorded in onboarding log.

---

## 3️⃣ Stage Trigger Conditions

Stage progression requires:

- Completion of prior stage documentation requirements
- Verification of role separation enforcement
- Parameter registry alignment validation
- Audit readiness confirmation
- Decision log integrity verification

Stage progression is not automatic.  
Stage progression requires explicit authorization by designated structural roles.

Authorization must be recorded in transition log.

---

## 4️⃣ Adoption Reversibility

### 4.1 Pause Capability

At any stage:

- Progression may be paused
- No additional tier coordination required
- Parameter updates may be temporarily suspended

Pause status MUST be logged.

---

### 4.2 Stage Reversion

Reversion to prior stage MUST:

- Restore prior parameter version references
- Preserve append-only decision logs
- Preserve audit logs
- Apply prospectively only

Historical records remain unchanged.

---

## 5️⃣ Partial Adoption Handling

### 5.1 Single-Unit Adoption

Individual units MAY operate under Stage 1 without cross-unit coordination.

---

### 5.2 Multi-Unit Coordination Without Tier Synchronization

Multiple units MAY coordinate parameters without activating higher-tier coordination.

---

### 5.3 Non-Participating Units

Units not participating in staged adoption:

- Retain existing governance structure
- Remain structurally unaffected
- Maintain independent parameter registry

No penalty or structural invalidation occurs.

---

## 6️⃣ Exit Conditions

Structured withdrawal MAY occur when:

- Governance framework is no longer applied within unit scope
- Parameter registry is archived
- Decision log retained
- Audit records preserved

Exit MUST:

- Preserve historical documentation
- Maintain parameter version history
- Avoid alteration of prior decisions

Withdrawal does not invalidate other participating units.

---

Institutional onboarding procedures operate within applicable legal and regulatory frameworks defined by participating institutions.

Adoption sequencing does not alter existing institutional authority structures.
