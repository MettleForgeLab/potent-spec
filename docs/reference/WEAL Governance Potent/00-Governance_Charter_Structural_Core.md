Governance Charter — Structural Core  
Scope: Structural definition of governance tiers, authority boundaries, and operational coordination framework for the Governance Coordination Potent.  
Version: 1.1 (Revised)

This file defines structural coordination only.  
It does not establish political federation, sovereignty, reform doctrine, or social contract theory.

---

## 1️⃣ Tier Definitions

### 1.1 Tier 1 — Local Coordination Unit (LCU)

**Scope of Responsibility**

- Maintain local parameter configurations within defined bounds
- Record coordination decisions affecting local operations
- Implement parameter updates authorized within scope
- Submit structured reports to Tier 2 (if applicable)

**Parameter Authority Boundaries**

- May adjust locally scoped parameters within inherited caps
- Must not override regional or cross-region caps
- Must not introduce new parameter namespaces outside declared schema

**Reporting Obligations**

- Maintain immutable local decision log
- Publish active parameter version identifiers
- Submit periodic coordination reports to Tier 2 where applicable

---

### 1.2 Tier 2 — Regional Coordination Unit (RCU)

**Scope of Responsibility**

- Coordinate parameter alignment across multiple Local Coordination Units
- Validate cross-local parameter consistency
- Maintain regional parameter registry

**Parameter Authority Boundaries**

- May define regional parameter constraints within cross-region caps
- Must not override cross-region maximum caps
- Must not alter local parameter history

**Reporting Obligations**

- Maintain regional change log
- Publish regional parameter versions
- Provide reconciliation documentation to Tier 3 (if applicable)

---

### 1.3 Tier 3 — Cross-Region Coordination Unit (CRCU)

**Scope of Responsibility**

- Define cross-region cap constraints
- Maintain cross-region parameter namespace registry
- Publish cross-region parameter versions

**Parameter Authority Boundaries**

- May define maximum caps applicable to Tier 2 and Tier 1
- Must not modify local or regional historical entries
- Must not impose retroactive parameter changes

**Reporting Obligations**

- Maintain cross-region parameter log
- Publish effective timestamps for parameter updates
- Document scope-level constraints clearly

No tier holds supremacy over ledger history.  
Tier relationships define scope layering only.

## 2️⃣ Role Structure

### 2.1 Functional Roles

Roles are structural designations.  
No identity-based or value-based assignment is defined.

Minimum role set:

- `Coordinator`
- `Auditor`
- `Maintainer`
- `Registrar`

---

### 2.2 Role Scope Limitations

**Coordinator**

- Proposes parameter adjustments within scope
- Cannot approve own proposals
- Cannot modify historical records

**Auditor**

- Reviews parameter compliance and logs
- Cannot initiate parameter changes
- Cannot modify ledger entries

**Maintainer**

- Implements approved parameter updates
- Cannot alter caps beyond defined limits
- Cannot bypass validation layers

**Registrar**

- Maintains version registry
- Records role assignments
- Ensures log retention integrity

No role may exercise unilateral control across proposal, approval, and audit functions.

---

## 3️⃣ Rotation Rules

### 3.1 Term Limits

If defined by deployment:

- Role terms MUST have declared duration
- Term length MUST be recorded in role registry

---

### 3.2 Rotation Cadence

- Role reassignment cadence MUST be declared
- Rotation events MUST be logged
- Interim appointments MUST follow documented procedure

---

### 3.3 Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility criteria MUST be:

- Structural
- Documented
- Scope-specific

No political or representational criteria are defined in this specification.

---

## 4️⃣ Anti-Capture Safeguards

### 4.1 Role Separation

The following separations are mandatory:

- Proposal role separate from approval role
- Approval role separate from audit role
- Audit role separate from implementation role

---

### 4.2 Cross-Role Approval Requirements

Parameter changes require:

- Proposal record
- Independent review confirmation
- Log registration prior to activation

---

### 4.3 Conflict Logging

All potential role conflicts MUST be:

- Declared in proposal record
- Logged in conflict registry
- Retained in audit log

---

### 4.4 Cap-Based Constraint Enforcement

No role may:

- Modify maximum caps beyond inherited scope
- Introduce parameters outside defined namespace
- Override cross-region cap constraints

---

### 4.5 Non-Overlapping Authority Rules

No single role or tier may:

- Propose
- Approve
- Implement
- Audit

the same change without independent separation.

---

## 5️⃣ Authority Boundaries

Governance tiers:

- Cannot modify historical ledger entries
- Cannot retroactively alter parameter effects
- Cannot override higher-scope cap constraints
- Cannot suspend validation requirements
- Cannot assume unilateral authority outside defined scope

No tier defines structural dominance over ledger integrity rules.

All parameter changes apply prospectively only.

---

## 6️⃣ Publication & Transparency Rules

### 6.1 Parameter Version Publication

Each active parameter set MUST include:

- Version identifier
- Effective timestamp
- Scope level
- Cap definitions

Historical versions MUST remain retrievable.

---

### 6.2 Decision Log Retention

Each tier MUST maintain:

- Immutable decision log
- Parameter change registry
- Role assignment record

Retention duration MUST be declared in deployment documentation.

---

### 6.3 Structured Documentation Requirements

All governance actions MUST include:

- Unique action identifier
- Role reference
- Timestamp
- Scope designation
- Parameter reference

Documentation format MUST be consistent across tiers.

---

### 6.4 Deployment-Defined Visibility Scope

Deployment documentation MUST define:

- Internal visibility scope
- External visibility scope
- Log export eligibility

Visibility scope does not alter structural authority boundaries.

---

Governance operations occur within applicable legal and regulatory frameworks defined by participating institutions.
